Quick start

You'll work in two places:

  • The Studio — letsbeready.sanity.studio — this is where you make changes. Think of it as the back office.
  • The Live Site — letsbeready.org — this is what visitors see. Your changes appear here automatically about 30 seconds after you publish them.

You won't ever need to touch code, files, or anything technical. Just click into a field, type, and hit Publish.

The 30-second rule After you click Publish in the Studio, give it about 30 seconds before refreshing the live site. The site rebuilds in the background — be a little patient and your changes will appear.

Logging in

  1. Open letsbeready.sanity.studio in your browser (Chrome, Safari, Firefox, Edge — all work).
  2. You'll be asked to sign in. Use the same email address that was used to invite you (Google sign-in works for Gmail and Google Workspace accounts).
  3. That's it — you're in. Bookmark the page so you can find it again.
Works on any device You can edit from a laptop, desktop, iPad, or even your phone. The studio is responsive and works in any modern browser. No app to install.

A tour of the studio

When you log in, you'll see a sidebar on the left. Each item is a different kind of content you can edit. Here's what each one does:

  • 📬 Subscribers — everyone who signed up for the newsletter. New ones appear at the top automatically.
  • Pages — the actual pages on the website. Click this to expand and see Home, Curriculum, Nutrition, Donate, Partners, and Staff.
  • Classrooms — every classroom that appears on the homepage map.
  • Team Members (leadership) — the leadership team that appears on the homepage.
  • Staff Members (full staff) — the broader staff list (teachers, coordinators) that appears on the optional Staff page.
  • Partners — the partner organizations on the Partners page.
  • Expense Allocation — the percentages that make up the financial pie chart on the homepage.
  • Site Settings — global stuff that appears everywhere (logo, contact email, social links, footer text). Set it once, it shows up on every page.

At the very top of the screen, you'll also see a Media tab — that's the photo library where every image lives. More on that below.

Wait — why can't I type? Drafts vs Published

This is the #1 source of confusion for new editors. When you open a document for the first time, the fields will look greyed out and you can't type into them. You're not broken — you're just looking at the live published version, which is read-only on purpose so nobody accidentally edits live content.

To enable editing, you need to switch from Published to Drafts:

  1. Look at the top-right of the Studio, in the very top bar. You'll see a button that says either Published (green dot) or Drafts (grey dot).
  2. Click that button — a small dropdown opens.
  3. Click Drafts.
  4. The fields are now editable. Make your changes.
  5. Click the Publish button in the bottom-right when you're done (more on this in the next section).

Why two modes?

  • Published view = what visitors currently see on the website. Locked. Look but don't touch.
  • Drafts view = your private editing workspace. Your changes here aren't visible to visitors until you click Publish.

You can switch between the two anytime. If you want to compare your draft to the live site, just toggle.

If you can't type, check the toggle Whenever a field seems "stuck" or read-only, your first move should be to look at the top-right corner. If it says Published, switch to Drafts and try again.

Note: there's also a + New release option in that dropdown. Ignore it. That's an advanced feature for scheduling batched changes — most editors will never need it.

The other golden rule: Publish

Once you've switched to Drafts and made your changes, the second thing you have to remember is:

Nothing goes live until you click PUBLISH When you change a field in Drafts mode, the studio saves your work automatically. But drafts are private — they don't appear on the website. You have to click the Publish button in the bottom-right of the document to push your changes live.

If you ever change something and it doesn't appear on the website, this is almost always why. Go back, check that you clicked Publish, and wait ~30 seconds for the site to update.

The Publish button looks slightly different depending on the state:

  • Highlighted "Publish" — there are unpublished changes. Click it.
  • Greyed "Published" — everything is up to date. Nothing to do.

Editing a page

Most of the website's content lives under Pages in the sidebar. Let's walk through editing one — say, the Curriculum page.

  1. Click Pages in the sidebar, then click Curriculum Page.
  2. You'll see tabs at the top: Hero, Intro, Pillars, Gardening, Nutritional Education, Supplement Highlight, CTA. These match the sections of the page in order.
  3. Click any tab and you'll see the fields inside it — headlines, body text, image slots, etc.
  4. Click into a field and edit it. The Studio auto-saves your draft as you type.
  5. When you're done, click the green Publish button in the top right.
  6. Wait ~30 seconds, then refresh letsbeready.org/curriculum to see the change.

The same pattern works for every page

Home Page, Nutrition Page, Donate Page, Partners Page, and Staff Page all work exactly the same way. Click the page → click a tab → edit the fields → publish.

Some fields say "HTML allowed" You can mostly ignore this. Just type your text. If you want to make a word italic, you can wrap it in <em>like this</em>. If you want a line break, use <br>. But plain text works fine for almost everything.

The two ⋯ menus on every document

When you open any document in the Studio, you'll notice two different three-dot (⋯) menus. They look similar but do completely different things. This trips everyone up at first, so it's worth a minute to learn.

Top ⋯ menu — upper-right of the page

This is the history & document tools menu. Most editors only need History; the rest are for occasional or advanced use.

  • History — opens a panel showing every saved version of this document over time. Pick a past version to compare or restore it. This is your undo button for text edits.
  • Compare versions — same idea, but only available once you've selected two versions in History to diff. Greyed out otherwise.
  • Create new task — assign a follow-up to a teammate (e.g., "double-check this bio"). Optional collaboration feature, no harm in ignoring it.
  • Copy document / Paste document — copy a whole document to the clipboard so you can paste it elsewhere. Editors won't usually need these.
  • Inspect — shows the raw underlying data. Developer-only — ignore.

Bottom ⋯ menu — next to the Publish button

This is the action menu. It does things to the document — and a couple of these are the only buttons in the Studio that can actually mess things up.

  • Link to Canvas — Sanity's AI editing tool. Ignore this. Editors don't need it.
  • Duplicate — makes a copy of the document with a new ID. Useful when you want to add a new person or item similar to an existing one. Duplicate it, change the name and details, then publish.
  • Unpublish — removes the document from the live site but keeps the draft in the Studio. Use this if you want to temporarily hide a page or item without deleting it. To bring it back, just click Publish again. Takes effect immediately.
  • Delete — permanently removes the document. There's a confirmation dialog, but once it's gone it's much harder to get back. For people, partners, classrooms — anything with an Active toggle — prefer toggling Active OFF instead of deleting. Same effect on the live site, but the document stays around.
Be deliberate with Delete and Unpublish These two take effect immediately on the live site. Text and photo edits are easy to roll back from History. Deleted documents are much harder to recover. When you're not sure, hide first (toggle Active OFF) and ask before clicking.

Adding or editing people

There are two separate lists of people:

  • Team Members (leadership) — the small group of leaders shown on the homepage (e.g., Garrett, Sara, Lucy, Fred).
  • Staff Members (full staff) — the broader staff list (teachers, field coordinators, cooks) shown on the optional Staff page.

Both work the same way. Here's how to add a new person:

  1. Click Team Members (or Staff Members) in the sidebar.
  2. Click the + Create button at the top right.
  3. Fill in the fields: Name, Role, Bio, and upload a Photo.
  4. Make sure Active is toggled ON. (Toggling it OFF hides the person without deleting them.)
  5. Set Display Order — lower numbers appear first. So 0 is first, 1 is second, and so on.
  6. Click Publish.

To remove someone

You have two options. Pick whichever fits:

  • Hide (recommended) — open the person, toggle Active to OFF, click Publish. They disappear from the website but stay in your list. Easy to bring back.
  • Delete (permanent) — open the person, click the ⋯ menu, choose Delete. Gone forever.
The grids on the website auto-flow You can add as many people as you want. The team grid on the homepage and the staff grid on the staff page automatically rearrange to fit, no matter how many people are in the list. You don't need to think about layout.

Adding photos

Anywhere you see an image field, you can upload a new photo or pick one from the media library.

  1. Click into the image field (e.g., a person's Photo, the homepage Hero Image, etc.).
  2. You'll see two options: Upload a new file from your computer, or Select from the media library (recommended if the photo is already there).
  3. Once the photo is in, you can click and drag the dot in the middle to set the focal point. This tells the site what to keep in the center when it crops for different screen sizes.
  4. Click Publish.
Photo size doesn't matter Upload the original full-resolution photo from your phone or camera. The site automatically resizes it for every device — small versions for phones, big versions for desktops. Don't waste time shrinking photos before uploading.

The media library

Every photo you've ever uploaded to the studio is stored in one place: the Media library. You can browse it, search it, tag photos, and reuse them across the entire site without re-uploading.

  1. At the top of the studio, click the Media tab.
  2. You'll see thumbnails of every uploaded photo.
  3. To upload more, just drag and drop them onto the page — you can do many at once.
  4. Click a photo to add tags (like "hero", "classroom", "team") or change its alt text and credit. This makes them easy to find later.
  5. To use a photo somewhere on the site, go to that page's image field and click Select — you'll see the same library and can pick any photo.
One photo, used many places The same photo can appear in multiple spots on the site — homepage, curriculum, partners — without being uploaded multiple times. Update it in the media library and it changes everywhere.

Newsletter signups

Whenever someone enters their email in the footer "Stay Updated" form, they show up in 📬 Subscribers at the top of the sidebar.

  1. Click 📬 Subscribers in the sidebar.
  2. The newest signups are at the top.
  3. Click any subscriber to see their email, signup date, and which page they came from.
  4. Use the Internal notes field to record things like "asked to unsubscribe" or "follow-up sent" — this is for your reference, donors don't see it.

Exporting the list

To get all subscribers as a CSV file (e.g., to import into Mailchimp or send a campaign):

  1. Open the Subscribers list.
  2. Click the ⋯ menu in the top right.
  3. Choose Export.
  4. You'll get a downloadable file with everyone's email and signup details.
This is your inbox, not a sender Sanity stores who signed up. It does not send the newsletter for you. You'll still need a service like Mailchimp, MailerLite, or Constant Contact to actually send emails. The subscribers list here is just where you keep track of who wants to hear from you.

The donate page & GiveLively

The donation form on the donate page is provided by GiveLively. It's embedded into our page, but the donation processing, receipts, and donor records all live in your GiveLively dashboard — not in Sanity.

Where donations go

  • To see donations: log into secure.givelively.org. Every donation appears there with name, amount, email, and date.
  • To download donor records: GiveLively dashboard → Data Reports → Export.
  • To change suggested donation amounts ($25, $50, $100, etc.): GiveLively dashboard → Donation Widgets → Simple Donation Widget → edit the suggested amounts → save. The widget on letsbeready.org will update automatically.

What you can edit on the donate page in Sanity

  • Hero — the headline, subheadline, photo, quote, and stats on the left side
  • Sponsor a Classroom card — the $3,800/year tier card
  • Lifetime Impact — the "$1.2M+ raised since 2008" stat block (currently placeholder — please update with real numbers from the annual report)
  • Where Your Money Goes — the percentage bars
  • Mail-a-check info — the address for paper check donations

The Sponsor a Classroom button

This button is intentionally not connected to GiveLively. When someone clicks it, it opens their email client with a pre-written message asking the org to get in touch. Sponsorships are major-gift conversations — you want a phone call with these donors, not a generic form submission.

Sponsor inquiries land in contact@letsbeready.org. Reply personally — these are your highest-value donors.

The Lifetime Impact numbers are placeholders The current values ($1.2M+, 3,400+, 180+) are made-up estimates. When you have real numbers from the annual report, edit them in Pages → Donate Page → Lifetime Impact tab. Each field is marked as a placeholder in the description.

Site Settings

Site Settings is where you change anything that appears on every page — the logo, contact email, social media links, footer text, etc. Update it once and it changes across the entire site.

  1. Click Site Settings at the bottom of the sidebar.
  2. You'll see tabs for Brand, Contact & Address, Social Links, SEO / Sharing, and Footer.
  3. Edit any field, then click Publish.

What's in each tab

  • Brand — the site name, logo image, favicon (the little icon in browser tabs)
  • Contact & Address — the contact email used in every "Contact Us" link and mail-to button across the site, and the physical address shown in the footer
  • Social Links — Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, and GlobalGiving URLs (used in the footer icons and the Donate page)
  • SEO / Sharing — the default image that appears when someone shares a link to letsbeready.org on Facebook, Twitter, etc.
  • Footer — the footer description text and the three trust pills ("Verified 501(c)(3)", "100% to Programs", "Since 2008")
Change the contact email in one place If your contact email ever changes, you only need to update it in Site Settings → Contact & Address. It will automatically update in the footer of every page, the donate page contact button, the sponsor a classroom email, and everywhere else it appears.

The classroom map

The interactive map on the homepage shows every classroom Let's Be Ready supports. Each pin is a real classroom you can click to see details.

To add a new classroom

  1. Click Classrooms in the sidebar.
  2. Click + Create.
  3. Fill in: ID (next available number), Community, Department, Teacher Name, Number of Students, Latitude, Longitude, and Year Started.
  4. Click Publish. The map updates in 30 seconds.
How to find latitude and longitude Open Google Maps, find the village, right-click on the spot, and click the coordinates that appear at the top of the menu — this copies them to your clipboard. Paste the first number into Latitude, the second into Longitude.

Things you don't have to worry about

Here's a list of things you might wonder about. Don't. They're all automatic:

  • Code, hosting, servers — handled.
  • Resizing photos — automatic. Upload the biggest version you have.
  • The website rebuilding — happens automatically every time you publish. You don't have to "deploy" anything.
  • Backups — Sanity keeps the full edit history of every document. You can roll back any change to any previous version.
  • Mobile responsiveness — the site automatically rearranges itself for phones, tablets, and desktops.
  • SSL / HTTPS — handled by the hosting platform.
  • Tax receipts for donors — handled by GiveLively automatically.
  • Spam in the newsletter signup — there's a hidden honeypot field that filters bots silently. You should very rarely see spam in your subscribers list.

Getting help

If something isn't working or you can't figure out where to edit something:

  • For Studio / website issues: contact your developer.
  • For donation / GiveLively issues: GiveLively support is at support@givelively.org. They're responsive and good.
  • For Sanity-specific questions: sanity.io/docs has plain-English guides for almost everything.
Most edits are reversible — but not all Text and photo edits can be rolled back from the top-right ⋯ → History menu. Deleted documents are much harder to recover, and Unpublish takes effect on the live site immediately. Be deliberate with those, and when in doubt, ask before clicking.
